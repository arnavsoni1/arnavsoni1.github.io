And he quite liked playing chess late at night. "Checkmate", was his favourite word. And his name, was Li Wei. Mr. Wei had an unparalleled interest in expanding business beyond people's wildest imaginations. Although how he got that rich was a closely guarded mystery, people knew it was not anything ethical. They were bought to Mr. Wei's Office in Shanghai. There were many tall buildings, just like New York. But something caught the eye of the old man. In a tall, abandoned building, on one of the windows, there was a man wearing a pair of sunglasses. Just after the old man saw him, he disappeared in the blink of an eye. The office of Li Wei was painted White from within, with green pillars. There was a huge chessboard in the middle, and in a corner office consisting of glass walls, a man with a tux sat waiting for them. "Welcome, to my world. The world of Wei", he said, as the old couple sat down on the light green couch. "I heard your daughter is searching frantically for you. What was her name again?", he asked, seemingly to no one. "Ah yes, Janet", he said, seemingly proud of answering himself. "If you dare drag her into this mess, you will regret it for the rest of your life", said the old man in an angry tone. He was certainly not Chinese, and he most certainly was not with Wei. The old man could feel it in his gut.
